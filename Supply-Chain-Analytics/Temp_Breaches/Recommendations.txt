Insights

Three districts – Bungoma, Migori, and Kisii – account for nearly all vaccine wastage, affecting Measles and BCG.

Bungoma has the highest rates: 32.3% for Measles, 20.3% for BCG.

In Migori, Measles wastage (10.8%) exceeds BCG (8.2%). In Kisii, only Measles shows significant wastage (11%).

Temperature breaches occur mostly in the afternoon (2 PM – 7 PM), aligning with the highest wastage events.

Recommendations

Reschedule deliveries – Shift BCG and Measles transport to early morning (before 10 AM) when ambient temperatures are lowest. This alone could reduce wastage by an estimated 60%.

Install low‑cost temperature alerts – Use simple thermometers with colour‑coded indicators or SMS alerts on vaccine boxes so drivers can react before vaccines spoil.

Right‑size deliveries – Provide demand‑driven quantities to avoid stockpiling, which reduces both wastage risk and storage pressure.

All recommendations are low‑cost, easy to implement, and sustainable within existing supply chain workflows.
